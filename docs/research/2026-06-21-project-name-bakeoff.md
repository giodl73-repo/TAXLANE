# Project Name Bakeoff

## Decision supported

INCOME-TAX was the working name. This bakeoff tests names against the emerging
project identity: a source-backed tax legibility standard that explains what
taxpayers pay, what the money is claimed to fund, and how a better public-purpose
lane system should work.

## Naming criteria

| Criterion | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Legibility | Signals clarity, receipts, lanes, or public understanding. |
| Scope fit | Covers more than income tax if the project grows into tax/public-finance transparency. |
| Neutrality | Avoids sounding partisan, anti-tax, or pro-tax by default. |
| Reform energy | Leaves room to advocate for better taxpayer-facing standards. |
| Portfolio fit | Works as a short public repo name. |

## Candidates

| Rank | Name | Score | Why it fits | Risk |
|---:|---|---:|---|---|
| 1 | TAXLANE | 9 | Best fit for the core idea: taxes should be visible as public-purpose lanes. Short, memorable, reform-oriented. | Could sound like it only models lane mechanics, not history. |
| 2 | RECEIPT | 8 | Captures the taxpayer-facing output: people should know what they paid for. Very legible. | Generic; may collide conceptually with ordinary receipts. |
| 3 | LEDGER | 8 | Strong accounting/trust signal; fits receipts, sources, and public finance records. | Too broad and likely used elsewhere. |
| 4 | TAXLEDGER | 8 | Clearer than LEDGER and directly tied to tax/accounting transparency. | Slightly dry; less advocacy energy than TAXLANE. |
| 5 | PUBLICPURSE | 7 | Classic civic phrase; captures public money and stewardship. | Longer; could imply all public finance, not income tax focus. |
| 6 | TAXPURPOSE | 7 | Directly expresses the standard: taxes should name their public purpose. | A bit abstract. |
| 7 | CIVICTAX | 7 | Good educational/civic feel; neutral. | Less specific about receipts and lanes. |
| 8 | PAYFOR | 6 | Plain-language hook: what are we paying for? | Too conversational; weak as a repo brand. |
| 9 | FUNDLINE | 6 | Signals funding lines/lanes. | Less obviously about taxes; could sound financial-tech. |
| 10 | INCOME-TAX | 5 | Accurate for the starting question and easy to understand. | Too narrow for the emerging standard and reform model. |

## Shortlist

### TAXLANE

Best current candidate. It names the reform thesis without overclaiming current
law: taxes can be explained as lanes, and some lanes may later become statutory
program-linked taxes.

Suggested tagline:

> TAXLANE is a source-backed standard for showing what taxpayers pay for, which
> public-purpose lanes their taxes support, and where current law differs from
> transparent reform.

### RECEIPT

Best public-facing output name. It works if the repo becomes mostly about a
taxpayer receipt standard rather than tax-lane reform.

Suggested tagline:

> RECEIPT shows taxpayers what their payments are claimed to fund, with clear
> labels for legal dedication, proportional allocation, and borrowed share.

### TAXLEDGER

Best accounting/source-custody name. It works if the repo becomes more about
historical rates, receipts, and outlay records than public advocacy.

Suggested tagline:

> TAXLEDGER records tax rates, receipts, outlays, and public-purpose allocations
> so tax debates start from reproducible evidence.

## Decision

Rename to **TAXLANE** now. The public-purpose lane thesis is strong enough to
make the narrower INCOME-TAX name misleading.

## Original rename trigger

The original trigger was to rename only after these exist:

1. OMB function to taxpayer lane crosswalk.
2. First taxpayer receipt method note.
3. First role review of the receipt method.
4. Decision that the repo is a tax legibility standard, not only an income-tax
   history/explainer.

The trigger is being pulled early because the user explicitly selected TAXLANE
and the repo has already adopted a lane taxonomy.
