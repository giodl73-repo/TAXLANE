# Panel Review: The Right Rate to Collect (Balanced-Budget Lane Design)

## What was reviewed

The proposed program-lane fair-rate model under a **hard balanced-budget rule**
("spend only what we collect, no borrowing"). All eight `.roles` lenses were run
on one question: what is the right total to collect, and how should the balanced
budget be allocated across lanes — cut vs collect more?

Inputs: FY2025 sourced baseline (outlays 7,011,105; receipts 5,236,421; deficit
1,774,684 $M; individual income tax 2,656,044), the 17-lane cost table, and the
international/historical benchmarks.

## Panel positions

| Role | Cut vs collect | Core principle | Decision label |
|---|---|---|---|
| T-1 Taxpayer Advocate | ~40 cut / 60 collect | Low-tax country; can't balance on waste cuts; cut health *price* not coverage | Collect-more / cut-the-price |
| T-2 Budget Accountant | ~45 cut / 55 collect | Balance each fund on its own receipts; ring-fence trust funds; fund interest off the top | Receipts/outlays/borrowing separation enforced |
| T-3 Source Custodian | (blocks a number) | No rate is "fair" without solvency + one of {historical, international}, each sourced | Rate-blocked pending receipts split + base |
| T-4 Public Goods Steward | ~60 cut / 40 collect | Sort public goods vs transfers vs debt first; fund non-excludable goods first; don't tax more to feed an over-priced lane | Purpose taxonomy unclear |
| T-5 Program Beneficiary | ~33 cut / 67 collect | Don't balance on beneficiaries' backs; mandatory shortfall/stabilizer rule | Shortfall rule missing |
| T-6 Compliance Burden | ~50 / 50 | Lanes live on the **receipt**, never as separately-filed taxes; base-broaden | Simpler receipt path preferred |
| T-7 Fiscal Sustainability | ~45 cut / 55 collect, phased 8-10 yr | Land at ~$6.0-6.2T, not $7.0T; debt-service-first; reserve rule; bend the cost curve | Sustainability visible |
| T-8 Reform Skeptic | range, no single number | A named rate is transparency, not control; bind appropriation/override or it's theater | Reform claim overstates evidence |

## Where the panel converges (high confidence)

1. **Two-sided gap.** No lens supports closing the $1.77T by cuts alone or
   revenue alone. Center of gravity: **~50-55% collect / ~45-50% cut**, with the
   cut concentrated on cost-efficiency, not benefit removal.
2. **The US is a low-tax country.** 25.6% tax-to-GDP vs 34.1% OECD (≈31st of 38).
   There is room to raise total collection toward the low-30s of GDP and still
   sit below the OECD average. Revenue should lead, but not alone.
3. **Health is the one true outlier — cut price, not coverage.** Unanimous. US
   government/compulsory health is ~14.3% of GDP, ~2x the OECD's 7.1%, for
   narrower coverage. Health + Medicare ≈ $1.98T (28% of outlays). This is a
   cost-per-outcome problem and the largest defensible spending lever.
4. **Net interest is senior and non-cuttable.** $970,065M (already larger than
   Defense). Fund it first; it is the strongest argument *for* the rule, and it
   shrinks as the primary deficit closes.
5. **Ring-fence Social Security and Medicare-HI.** They run on dedicated payroll
   tax; "balancing the general fund by spending the payroll surplus" is a
   trust-fund raid, not discipline. Carve them into self-balancing lanes.
6. **Protect the social floor.** US public social spending (19% of GDP) and family
   benefits (<1% vs OECD 2.35%) are already below peers — not where an
   outlier-convergence cut belongs.

## Where the panel divides (the real decisions)

- **Cut/collect mix.** Public Goods Steward leans restraint (~60% cut); Program
  Beneficiary leans revenue (~67% collect). The honest output is a **range**, not
  a point (Reform Skeptic): ~$5.24T (cut-to-receipts, brutal) to ~$7.01T
  (collect-to-outlays, ~34% revenue rise). Most lenses cluster near a phased
  landing around **$6.0-6.3T**.
- **Pace.** Fiscal Sustainability: a one-year close is a ~6%-of-GDP shock; use an
  8-10 year legislated corridor plus an over-the-cycle reserve, or the rule forces
  pro-cyclical austerity.

## Two design corrections the panel forces

1. **Lanes are receipt-side display, not separately-collected taxes.** (Compliance
   Burden + Reform Skeptic.) Splitting the income tax into N filed "lane rates"
   multiplies withholding, reporting, and reconciliation by N for zero legibility
   gain over showing proportional shares of one liability. **One return, one base;
   show the lanes on the receipt.** Social Security/Medicare-HI remain the only
   genuinely separate collected lanes (and that one split already costs a whole
   parallel FICA system).
2. **A balance rule needs guardrails or it is theater.** (Reform Skeptic.) Bind
   `appropriation_rule`, `shortfall_rule`, `surplus_rule`, `override_rule` with
   supermajority gates; independent **actual-receipts** trigger (not forecast);
   hard emergency-designation limits; an always-visible `deficit_gap` lane; and a
   published forecast-vs-actual reconciliation. Named evasions to block: optimistic
   baselines, timing shifts, off-budget vehicles, emergency designation, surplus
   sweeps, silent shortfall backfills, tax-expenditure laundering, entitlement
   autopilot, rate-base flight, and "it balances" laundering.

## Data gates before any statutory rate (Source Custodian, P-blocking)

- **FY2025 receipts-by-source split is MISSING** — 2,580,377 of receipts is an
  unlabeled residual. No "collect more from corporate/payroll/excise" claim is
  sourced yet. (Wave Pulse 02.)
- **Aggregate AGI/payroll base is MISSING** — no statutory "X% of income" rate can
  be stated; only receipt-share framing is defensible now. (Wave Pulse 03.)
- International anchors are **external comparators** (year-drifted; federal-vs-all-
  government scope hazard), usable to bound rates, not to assert findings.
- The anti-waste thesis remains an **argued claim** (0/2 accountability records
  cleared), not a finding.

## Synthesized panel stance (for the rate model)

- **Destination**: a balanced budget phased over ~8-10 years, landing near
  **$6.0-6.3T**, with total tax-to-GDP rising from 25.6% toward the **low-30s**
  (still below OECD average).
- **Mix**: **~55% collect / ~45% cut-and-bend.** Revenue via base-broadening and
  better withholding on one unified income tax; the cut concentrated on the
  **health cost-per-outcome** excess and **defense toward the ~2-3.5% band**.
- **Protect**: net interest (senior), the social floor (below peers), Social
  Security/Veterans near-term promises; ring-fence the payroll-funded lanes.
- **Form**: lanes as receipt-side shares of one return; statutory guardrails on
  appropriation/shortfall/surplus/override; deficit_gap always visible.

## Acceptance status (per ROLE.md acceptance rule)

Budget Accountant and Reform Skeptic both require the allocation method explicit
and fungibility/deficit caveats visible. The current receipt-share model labels
`allocation_method = proposed_reform` and keeps the deficit visible — **method and
caveat conditions met** — but Reform Skeptic and Source Custodian raise **P1/P2**
items (guardrails undefined; receipts split + base missing) that block any
*statutory* rate claim until Pulses 02-03 and a guardrail spec land.
