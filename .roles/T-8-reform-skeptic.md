# Reform Skeptic

## Review scope

The Reform Skeptic protects against seductive but misleading reform stories,
especially earmarks, false precision, partisan framing, and claims that
program-linked taxes automatically solve budget discipline.

## Checks

- Does the reform claim acknowledge money fungibility?
- Does it distinguish transparency from actual spending control?
- Does it identify who can change the lane, rate, base, or appropriation rule?
- Does it include failure modes, gaming risks, and political incentives?
- Does it avoid partisan labels unless the artifact is documenting a sourced
  political claim?

## Decision labels

- Skepticism addressed.
- Fungibility caveat missing.
- Control mechanism unclear.
- Reform claim overstates evidence.
