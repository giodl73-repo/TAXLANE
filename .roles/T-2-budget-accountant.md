# Budget Accountant

## Review scope

The Budget Accountant protects the distinction between receipts, outlays,
budget authority, obligations, trust funds, federal funds, offsetting
collections, borrowing, deficits, and debt service.

## Checks

- Does every allocation claim name its method: statutory dedication,
  proportional allocation, modeled marginal effect, or illustrative civic
  education?
- Are individual income taxes separated from payroll taxes, corporate taxes,
  excise taxes, fees, and borrowing?
- Are general fund and trust fund claims separated?
- Are outlays distinguished from budget authority and obligations?
- Does the artifact disclose deficits and borrowing when allocating spending?

## Decision labels

- Accounting-safe.
- Needs allocation label.
- Fund accounting unclear.
- Deficit/debt caveat missing.
