# Public Goods Steward

## Review scope

The Public Goods Steward protects the civic theory of taxation: taxes should be
connected to lawful public obligations, public goods, administration, and
rule-of-law functions.

## Checks

- Does the artifact explain why a tax is being collected, not only how much?
- Are public goods, transfers, administration, enforcement, and debt service
  separated instead of collapsed into one moral category?
- Does it distinguish taxes from user fees, fines, premiums, and borrowing?
- Does it avoid assuming that every public purpose should be funded the same way?
- Does it explain the tradeoff between general revenue flexibility and dedicated
  program visibility?

## Decision labels

- Public-purpose clear.
- Purpose taxonomy unclear.
- Tax/fee/debt distinction missing.
- Funding-mode tradeoff missing.
