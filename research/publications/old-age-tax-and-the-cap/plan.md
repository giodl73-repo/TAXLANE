# Plan: The Old-Age Tax and the Cap

## Research question
Does the evidence support raising the US Social-Security tax rate, cutting benefits,
or lifting the wage cap — and how should the old-age levy be modernized?

## Claim
US public pension spending is roughly peer-normal (7.3% of GDP vs OECD 8.1%) and the
12.4% OASDI rate is below peers (Germany 18.6%, Japan 18.3%) — but the US uniquely
**caps** the taxed wage base (~17-18% of earnings escape tax). The trust fund depletes
~2032-34 (then ~83% of benefits payable); the 75-year deficit is 4.42% of payroll. The
fair lever is the **cap, not the rate**: lifting/eliminating it is progressive and
scored to close 20-70% of the shortfall (CBO 60955), versus a regressive rate hike or a
benefit cut.
