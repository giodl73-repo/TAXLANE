# Plan: Legible Federal Funding (T0 Synthesis)

## Research question
Tying the five accepted tracks together: is the US income tax "too high" or "too low,"
and what would a legible, program-lane funding system show that a single tax number hides?

## Claim (synthesis, not a new derivation)
There is **no single answer** to "is the tax too high or too low," because the US funds
its major lanes on **five different logics**, and the one-line "income tax" label hides
all of them:
- **Health (T1):** over-funded vs peers — ~14.3% of GDP (govt+compulsory) vs OECD 7.1%,
  ~2× for worse outcomes; lever = **price-per-outcome**, not the rate.
- **Social Security (T2):** peer-normal spend (~7.3% vs OECD 8.1%) on a **below-peer rate
  (12.4%) and a low, eroding capped base**; lever = **the cap**, not the rate.
- **Defense (T3):** above the European norm (~3.0–3.4% vs ~2.0%) but a **strategic
  judgment**, expressed as a **band (2.0–3.5% of GDP)**, not a fiscal finding.
- **Income Security (T4):** **under-funded** family support (~1.1% vs OECD ~2.4% of GDP);
  direction = **strengthen + stabilize**.
- **Revenue/Solvency (T5):** the US **under-collects overall** — 25.6% of GDP (all-gov)
  vs OECD 34.1% — and **borrows ~25% of what it spends** ($1.77T FY2025 gap).

Synthesis thesis: the US **over-funds one lane (health price), under-funds another
(family), funds defense by strategy, runs a structurally-capped old-age lane, and
under-collects overall** — so the reform is **lane-specific**, and the contribution is a
**legibility standard**: show each lane's level, its correct comparison basis, its
borrowed share, and its outcome — not a single number.

## The hardest problem (the gate)
The per-lane **%-of-GDP** figures are on **different bases** and are **NOT additive / NOT
rank-comparable**: health 14.3% = government+compulsory (incl. private); SS 7.3% = OECD
public pension; defense 3.0% = OMB function 050; family ~1.1% = SOCX public + TBSP;
revenue 25.6% = all-government taxes. The synthesis must (a) present **internal budget
shares** (% of FY2025 outlays, all OMB-function, **additive**) separately from
(b) **international comparisons** (per-basis, **not additive**), and state non-additivity
explicitly. Internal shares (additive, `SRC-OMB-HIST-3-2-FY2027`):
Health+Medicare 28.2% · Social Security 22.5% · Defense 13.1% · Income Security 10.0%
(+ net interest ~3.2% of GDP, rivaling defense, `SRC-CBO-LTBO`).

## Honesty hooks
- Non-additivity warning is THE gate item; never sum the %-GDP column.
- Keep each lane's normative direction a **separate value judgment** — do not package
  five directions as one ideology (PF-6 risk); legibility ≠ a spending verdict.
- Fungibility caveat: a "lane" is a legibility device, not a legal earmark.
- The synthesis **cites**, does not re-derive; every restated figure carries its origin
  paper's SRC ID.
