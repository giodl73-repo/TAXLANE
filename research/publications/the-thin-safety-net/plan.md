# Plan: The Thin Safety Net

## Research question
Does the evidence support raising, holding, or cutting US income-security and family
funding — and how should the safety-net lane be modernized?

## Claim
US income security (OMB **function 600**) cost **$701,609M in FY2025 — 10.0% of federal
outlays** (~2.3% of GDP) [`SRC-OMB-HIST-3-2-FY2027`, `SRC-OMB-HIST-1-2-FY2027`]. On the
clean like-for-like OECD comparison the US is **well below the norm on family support**:
**public family benefits ~0.6% of GDP vs the OECD ~2.4%** [`SRC-OECD-SOCX`]. (Honest
scope concession: on **total** public social spending the US is only slightly below
gross — 19.8% vs 21.2% — and on a **net-total** basis including mandatory private
spending the US is near the top; the "below the norm" claim is specific to **family /
working-age** support, not all social spending.) The US delivers much of its family
support through the **tax code** (refundable EITC/CTC) rather than durable direct
benefits [`SRC-CBO-TAX-EXP-2025`]. The 2021 expanded Child Tax Credit cut **SPM child
poverty to a record 5.2%**, and its expiry let child poverty **more than double to 12.4%
in 2022** [`SRC-CENSUS-P60-280`]. Direction: **strengthen and stabilize**, not cut — and
the modernization case is as much about **durability and delivery** (stop-go tax credits
vs steady benefits) as about the level.

## Evidence (ledger-sourced)
- Income-security cost / % outlays: `SRC-OMB-HIST-3-2-FY2027` (function 600 = $701,609M).
- GDP denominator: `SRC-OMB-HIST-1-2-FY2027`.
- Public social + family-benefit % GDP (US vs OECD): `SRC-OECD-SOCX`.
- SPM child poverty + 2021 CTC episode: `SRC-CENSUS-P60-280`.
- Tax-code delivery (EITC/CTC as refundable credits / tax expenditures): `SRC-CBO-TAX-EXP-2025`.

## Honesty hooks (baked in to clear round 1)
- **Scope labels, the gate:** never compare OMB federal function 600 to OECD
  all-government SOCX; the cross-country claim rests on the **family-benefit** category
  (public, % GDP), and concede the total-social-spend and net-total caveats up front.
- Direction is an **increase** — be explicit it is a normative/value judgment (a
  poverty-reduction premise), not a derived finding; show the cost.
- Distribution/incidence: the safety net is the most **progressive** spending lane —
  benefits concentrate at the bottom; quantify with the 2021→2022 SPM swing.
- Forward trajectory: the stop-go CTC history is the trajectory; durability is the point.
- Fungibility/lane label: function 600 is general-funded; refundable credits blur the
  tax/outlay line — label allocation_method.
