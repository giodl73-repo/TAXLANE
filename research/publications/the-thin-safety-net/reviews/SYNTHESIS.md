## P1s for round 2

1. **Gate fail — the family-benefit gap is overstated** (PF-2). OECD SOCX "public family
   benefits" undercounts the US's **tax-code delivery** (refundable EITC/CTC are
   classified as tax measures, not family benefits). Net the tax-code support in: add the
   EITC/refundable-CTC magnitude (~0.4–0.5% of GDP), show US family support is closer to
   ~1–1.1% of GDP, and state the gap **narrows from ~4× to ~2× but does not close**
   (still well below ~2.4%). Cite `SRC-CBO-TAX-EXP-2025` / `SRC-TREASURY-OTA-TE`.
2. **Gate fail — CTC causality overclaim** (PF-6). §5 attributes the entire 5.2%→12.4%
   child-poverty swing to the Child Tax Credit; 2021 also had stimulus payments and
   expanded UI, and 2022 lost all of them amid high inflation. Reframe as "the clearest
   single piece of evidence, not a controlled experiment," attribute a **large share**
   (not all) to the CTC, and name the confounders.
3. **Pin the OECD figures** (PF-7, conditional gate). Present 0.6/2.4 and 19.8/21.2 as
   the OECD-reported SOCX-2022 values, or label "approximate — exact extraction pending."
