# Review — PF-6 (Political Economy & Reform Skeptic, Round 1)

**Score: 2.0 — major revisions. GATE: FAIL.**

The normative framing is handled well — the strengthen-not-cut direction is explicitly
labeled a value judgment, the work-incentive tradeoff is flagged, and the durability
point is genuinely fresh. **But §5 overclaims causality, and that is a gate problem.**
The paper calls the 2021→2022 swing a "natural experiment" and attributes the child-
poverty doubling to the **Child Tax Credit**. In fact 2021 stacked **multiple** pandemic
supports — the expanded CTC, **stimulus/economic-impact payments, and expanded
unemployment insurance** — and 2022 lost **all** of them at once, against a backdrop of
high **inflation**. The expanded CTC was a **major** contributor but not the **sole**
cause; a clean controlled experiment it is not. **P1 (gate):** stop attributing the whole
5.2%→12.4% swing to the CTC. Reframe as "the clearest single piece of evidence, though
not a controlled experiment," cite that analysts attribute a **large share** (not all) to
the CTC specifically, and acknowledge the confounders. The reform case (full
refundability) does **not** require the overclaim — the CTC-specific effect is large
enough on its own — so fixing this strengthens, not weakens, the paper. P2: "the US chose
not to sustain it" is rhetorically loaded; keep it, but pair it with the work-incentive
counterargument you already flag.
