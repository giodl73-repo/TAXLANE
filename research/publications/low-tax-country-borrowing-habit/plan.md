# Plan: A Low-Tax Country with a Borrowing Habit

## Research question
Is the US FY2025 deficit a spending problem, a revenue problem, or both — and what
revenue direction do peer + historical + structural factors support?

## Claim
The US is among the lowest-taxed rich democracies (25.6% of GDP, all government, vs
the OECD's 34.1%). The deficit is as much a revenue gap as a spending excess: federal
receipts sit at their 50-year norm (~17.3% of GDP) while outlays run above theirs.
The fair direction is to raise revenue toward solvency, blended with spending
discipline — even full revenue-side closure leaves the US near 31% of GDP, below the
OECD average. Modernizing the revenue *base* (not just rates) is the structural lever.
