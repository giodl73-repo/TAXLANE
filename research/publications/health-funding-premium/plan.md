# Plan: The American Health Funding Premium

## Research question
Does the evidence (cost, history, peer benchmark, all factors) support raising or
cutting the US health-funding rate — and what would it take to modernize how the
US funds health?

## Claim
The US over-funds health on price, not coverage: government/compulsory health is
~14.3% of GDP vs the OECD's 7.1% for narrower coverage. The fair move is an
efficiency-first health lane that bends cost-per-outcome down (10/20/30% scenarios
free $198B/$395B/$593B) under a binding coverage floor — not a rate increase to
feed a 2x-cost system.
