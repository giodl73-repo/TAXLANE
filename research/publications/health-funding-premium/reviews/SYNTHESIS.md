## Convergent findings (multiple reviewers)

1. **Mechanical savings presented as bankable deficit reduction** — PF-1, PF-3, PF-4
   all flag this as P1. The $198B–$593B are flat percentages of lane cost with no
   behavioral model, no causal link to the funding-rate lever, while §8 concedes the
   mechanism is out-of-scope and historically fails. *The strongest cross-cutting
   blocker.*
2. **Gate fail — derived records not in the ledger** (PF-7, P1). The
   `income_tax_outlay_model` decade-summary and `health_efficiency_scenarios` tables
   are cited but have no ledger provenance, violating the repo's no-unsourced-table
   rule.
3. **No outcomes/coverage quantification** (PF-3) — the cost-per-*outcome* thesis
   never cites an outcome (uninsured rate, amenable mortality).
4. **No multi-year trajectory / aging driver** (PF-4) — health's long-run-driver
   status rests on demographics, which the paper omits; "unit cost not caseload"
   is misleading.
5. **No distributional incidence** (PF-5) — who bears the premium (HI payroll vs
   general vs private) and who captures the savings is absent from a paper making a
   normative funding recommendation.

## Affirmed strengths

- Benchmark scopes matched cleanly (PF-2 gate pass) — the most-abused move in this
  literature is avoided.
- The reform claim stays inside its evidence (PF-6 gate pass) — savings demoted to
  conditional, fungibility conceded.
- Arithmetic reconciles exactly (PF-7) — the problem is sourcing custody, not math.

## Decision

Stage: **panel, round 1 complete → major revisions**. Proceed to Round 2 after the
REVISION-PLAN items, prioritizing the gate-blocking P1s (mechanical-savings reframe;
ledger registration of derived records).
