# Review — PF-4 Fiscal Sustainability Economist (Round 1)

**Score: 2.0 — major revisions.**

Summary: core empirical claim well-anchored, but the solvency analysis is a single
FY2025 snapshot in trajectory language, and "closes 11–33% of the deficit" risks
treating speculative savings as realized cash.

Strengths: honest borrowed-share framing; health-as-general-revenue insight; coverage-floor gate.

- P1: No multi-year trajectory and no aging/demographic driver — the single most important reason health is the long-run cost driver. "Unit cost, not caseload" (§3/§6) is incomplete to the point of misleading: enrollment growth from aging is a primary structural driver.
- P2: "closes 11–33% of the deficit" books speculative savings as cash while §8 concedes the maneuver is failure-prone; remove from abstract/conclusion or label conditional.
- P3: efficiency frees a one-time level, not a sustained path; against a rising cost curve the saving erodes within years.

TOP_P1: no multi-year trajectory or aging driver — long-run solvency asserted on a static snapshot.
