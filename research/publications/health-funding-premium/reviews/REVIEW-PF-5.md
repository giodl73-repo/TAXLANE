# Review — PF-5 Distribution & Equity Economist (Round 1)

**Score: 2.0 — major revisions.**

Summary: competent aggregate fiscal incidence but silent on who pays the premium and
who gains or loses from squeezing it — the distributional question this lens exists to ask.

Strengths: equity-protective coverage-floor rule; honest HI-payroll vs general-revenue disaggregation (raw material for an incidence analysis never performed).

- P1: No incidence analysis. The premium's financing is regressive-to-mixed (HI payroll 2.9% uncapped; general revenue; compulsory private falling hard on lower-income insured). A welfare-improving direction can't be claimed without burden-by-income-decile.
- P2: "efficiency-first" is distributionally non-neutral but treated as neutral — a price squeeze is lost revenue to providers, including safety-net/rural/Medicaid-dependent systems; the floor protects enrollment count, not the income gradient of access.
- P3: beneficiaries not distinguished (Medicare seniors vs Medicaid enrollees face opposite incidence).

TOP_P1: no distributional incidence analysis for a paper making a normative funding-rate recommendation.
